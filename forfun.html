Roshni's bookshelf: read

Guns, Germs, and Steel: The Fates of Human Societies
by Jared Diamond
Why did the Europeans conquer the Native Americans and not the other way around? The answer has to do with domesticated plants/animals and even the orientations of the continents. Diamond writes an almost conversational history of humans...
tagged: nonfiction
A Grave in Gaza
by Matt Rees
Not much of a mystery but more a spotlight onto the spiderweb of Palestine. People are not good or bad, heroes or villains, but are simply desperate, occasionally outwardly ideological, but always fighting for survival.
tagged: culture-history and mystery
Death at Wentwater Court
by Carola Dunn
One of those classic British country manor deaths. The protagonist is a young Ms Marple equivalent, and "has one of those manners where people confide in her" that many female protagonists happen to have. An enjoyable and quick read.
tagged: historical-mystery
Blandings Castle
by P.G. Wodehouse
Short stories with a mix of Emsworth and Mulliner
tagged: comedy-and-humor
The Shadow Collector
by Kate Ellis
The way she weaves three different mysteries together: historical, a few years ago, and the present is very well done, and the parallels she draws between them just enhances the book even more. Overall, an entertaining read.
tagged: historical-mystery
